Polar H10: Why chest-strap accuracy still matters

Published February 25, 2026

When athletes move from casual training to targeted performance work, data quality starts to matter more than data quantity. A chest strap like the Polar H10 remains valuable because it captures fast heart-rate changes more reliably than most wrist-based sensors during hard sessions.

Where better accuracy helps

  • Intervals: Faster response gives cleaner feedback when you are trying to hold a precise effort.
  • Threshold sessions: Stable heart-rate trends make it easier to avoid drifting too hard.
  • Recovery validation: Reliable readings improve confidence when reviewing how quickly heart rate settles after work blocks.

Better signal quality also supports cleaner HRV collection workflows. It will not replace smart coaching decisions, but it reduces the chance of making decisions based on noisy inputs.
