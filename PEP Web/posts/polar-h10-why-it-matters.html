Why Polar H10 is the required sensor for PeakElite

Updated February 25, 2026

PeakElite makes same-day training guidance from a morning resting baseline. If the signal is noisy, readiness and load estimates become less useful. That is why Polar H10 is currently the required and supported sensor.

What this improves

  • More stable RR capture for baseline trend tracking.
  • Cleaner readiness estimates when day-to-day changes are small.
  • Higher confidence when deciding whether to keep or reduce planned load.

Wrist devices can be useful for general monitoring, but PeakElite relies on a reliable baseline input first, then adds optional context.
